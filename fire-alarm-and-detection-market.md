<p><p>The fire alarm and detection market comprises various systems tailored for safety. Conventional systems use zone-based technology for identifying fire locations, while addressable systems offer individual device identification for precise alerts. Flame detectors respond to specific wavelengths emitted by flames, ensuring quick detection of fires. Smoke detectors sense airborne particles to detect fires early, while heat detectors monitor temperature changes, triggering alarms upon reaching a predefined threshold. Each type serves distinct environments, enhancing overall fire safety and response.</p></p>
