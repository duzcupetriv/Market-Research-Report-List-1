<p><strong>The Boat Insurance Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Actual Cash Value</li><li>Agreed Amount Value</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Boat insurance typically features two market types: Actual Cash Value (ACV) and Agreed Amount Value. ACV coverage insures the boat for its current market value, factoring in depreciation, meaning the payout reflects the boat’s worth at the time of loss. In contrast, Agreed Amount Value covers the vessel for a pre-determined amount agreed upon by both the insurer and the insured, ensuring that in the event of a total loss, the owner receives that fixed value regardless of market fluctuations.</p></p>
